VELA

WEATHER FORECAST

Vela is your customized weather forecast app focused on water sports that helps you stay informed before conditions arrive.

Whether you’re into ​sailing,​ ​surfing,​ ​diving ​or any other​ ​water​ ​sports​, Vela helps alert you of current conditions and allows you to plan ahead when viewing a location so you are prepared.

I created this project for my professional development course at CareerFoundry.

Sneak Peak of Vela

GOAL

An app which shows at a glance and in an appealing informative design the current weather forecast and​ ​statistics​ ​for​ ​sailors,​ ​surfers,​ ​divers​ ​and​ ​any​ ​other​ ​water​ ​sport​ ​aficionado.

PROBLEM

How to design a weather forecast app that has easy-to-comprehend​ ​data​ ​displays,​ ​figuring​ ​out​ ​ways​ ​to encourage​ ​users​ ​to​ ​get​ ​in​ ​the​ ​habit​ ​of​ ​checking​ ​the​ ​app​ ​before​ ​heading​ ​out​ ​to​ ​the​ ​water,​ ​and creating an attractive user interface.​​​​​​​

MY ROAL

Being part of a 5-month CareerFoundry intensive user experience course, I was the product owner of the whole strategy, responsible for user experience research, information architecture, user testing, user interface, visual design and branding.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

During this study phase for Vela a competitive analysis was completed to get to know the competitors. It became clear a few other wind and weather forecast suppliers. If you want to know more about a big player check my competitor analysis of the Windy app.

STRENGTHS

  • Its competence for wind, waves and weather
  • The possibility to define pre-sets for specific sports
  • Optimized data transfer to ensure quicker downloads on the go from any mobile device

WEAKNESS

  • If you’re new to the wind, waves and weather forecasts, the visualizations and informations are quite overwhelming, but given the time, you’ll understand it eventually
  • Their social media channels need some fresh air and a straight line and there are other forecast apps which have a better user interface and user experience as well as iconography

OPPORTUNITIES

  • If they would do more on their social media channels - for example some cooperation with professional athletes – they could generate more traffic
  • Improve the loading time
  • Reduce the amount of ads that pop up
  • Improve the design to reduce confusion when scrolling through the app

THREATS

  • There are some competitors which have a much better social media performance like AccuWeather or PredictWind
  • On their blog or social media channels they work with professional water sports athletes
  • They show their top surfing spots or how to stay cool in times like these
  • The user interface as well as the usability is more appealing and easier to understand

BUSINESS REQUIREMENTS

TARGET AUDIENCE

Vela’s target audience is actually everyone who loves the combination of wind, water and sports. This could be sailors,​ ​surfers,​ ​divers​ ​and​ ​any​ ​other​ ​watersports​ ​aficionado​, …

My target group is very open minded, likes the ability to search / favorite their perfect routes or surf schools in any area, to view live webcams while reading comments about the area from other users of the app, and easy to read wind and wave forecast graphics for any day of week. As the app covers a very wide user base, both in age range as in income, the target group consist of people from 16 to 99+.

COMPETITION

There are a few really good competitors like AccuWeather or PredictWind. Both are very popular and established. With AccuWeather, you can always get the most accurate weather forecasts with real-time alerts. Unsuitable alerts can also be disabled or hidden. Sun or rain or wind or whatever it may be, AccuWeather gives you the information right away. It is very much accurate, and hence information from AccuWeather can be trusted. It also offers daily temperature details, local weather information, as well as a global weather forecast.

PredictWind is an excellent solution to the cruising sailor’s basic need - reliable knowledge of the weather at sea. Plus it does a remarkable job of providing weather routing.

But apps like Windy, AccuWeather or PredictWind have one big problem. Their forecast information is really complicated and overwhelming for inexperienced users.

RISK / OPPORTUNITY

While other apps aren’t using their full potential, they have a good market penetration. Vela might not be able to gain market shares fast enough to win users before they react.

Existing apps however focus on certain sports or at least a narrow niche. Vela could use its approach of combining all water based sports and create a meta community by diffusing and softening borders.

CONCLUSION

Vela needs to stand out with detailed and easily understandable forecasts.

It has to offer a variety of activities and needs to know their specific requirements.

Usability and user interface of the app as well as of the website have to be on point.

Vela has to create an appealing social media presence including stunning pictures and fascinating blogposts and on Instagram/Facebook to get in touch with their community.

Users need to be able to share their own stories and pictures directly via the app, tagging the Vela community.

USER INTERVIEWS & RESEARCH ANAYSIS

It’s very important to understand who will be using the product. In order to solve a real user problem, you need to have a clear problem statement in mind; in order to write this problem statement, you first need to understand your users and their needs. This was accomplished by interviews with potential future users and completing a user research analysis.

I created user personas to understand what my users really need.

What is their main barrier to achieving this goal?

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

USER FLOWS & SITE MAPS

Sitemaps are a common and important tool in the information architecture and an integral part in user experience. To help build these maps user flows were created for each persona. The structure of Vela has co-existing hierarchies. I analyzed other websites’ structure and have derived and developed my structure from it.

As I moved on creating my business requirement document, I documented all app components in a diagram. This helped defining my sitemap and the general app structure.

I verified my app structure via card sorting with a group of fifteen users and it was confirmed to be on point. After the usability test I renamed some areas though.

CONCLUSION

I enjoyed the process of ux design to create a significant and useful experience of Vela for a group of specific users. There are many different factors and elements that should be taken into consideration to achieve great quality. I found data gathering from usability testing especially useful and rewarding. CURRENT HYPOTHESIS

I believe that a free trial for three months will show how Vela is doing on the market and can help to grow their business. I know that giving a free trial will stimulate more people to create accounts and allow them to experience the full service world of Vela.

In this time I conduct another round of user tests and surveys to understand the user’s mind and how to improve the app.

In the meantime I will not stand still, I will improve Vela’s usability as well by new features and A/B Tests.

IMPROVEMENTS

One improvement is to remove the burger menu on the upper left. I think it doesn’t help to put these functions on an equal level with those from the bottom navigation. Also would like to show more data on the wind reports screen with the use of icons.
